Disney Parks & Experiences

Guest Service Suite

Simplifying how Cast Members assist guests across all Disney parks—replacing 60+ disconnected apps with one responsive, unified experience.

Role

Lead Product Designer

Timeline

May 2019 – Dec 2020

Team

2 → 4 Designers

Platform

Responsive Web

The Challenge

60+ apps, paper documents, and frustrated Cast Members

Disney staff struggled with efficiency issues helping guests in the parks—causing guest frustration and complaints. Cast Members had to navigate multiple applications, use paper documents, and rely on manual processes to assist guests pre-arrival, on-site, and post-visit.

Device constraints diagram

Challenge 01

Not Responsive, Too Many Steps

Over 60% of in-park staff only had access to mobile or tablet devices, but tools weren't built for them. Incident reports required completing 11 dropdown selections—some with hundreds of options.

Challenge 02

Limited Information & Communication

Guest and park information was unavailable or scarce. Staff relied on paper flyers for daily events and the guest's own app for a clear picture. Urgent updates were difficult to communicate.

Challenge 03

60+ Disconnected Apps

Staff used over 60 guest service apps to do their jobs, jumping between them to see guest info and take action. Guest satisfaction was difficult to improve without accurate reporting.

Inform

Ensure staff has access to guest and park information they need to help guests.

Simplify

Provide staff with a better view of guest information and quick access from multiple systems.

Empower

Allow more staff to assist guests in real-time regardless of their device.

Who We're Helping

Cast Members across every touchpoint

Our product focused on staff members who interacted with guests regularly through all Disney parks—from in-park merchandise coordinators to call center specialists to guest experience managers.

The Solution

One responsive app to replace them all

We combined functionality from several legacy applications into four main sections: Guest Interaction Tracking, Guest Itinerary & Scheduling, Tickets & Media, and Admin. The interface works seamlessly across devices, from desktop to mobile.

Interaction history view

Feature

Guest Interaction History

I led the design on Guest Interaction Tracking—giving Cast Members the ability to log new interactions and review guest history. The UI displays information at a glance and allows Cast to initiate actions easily to resolve issues.

Multiple view options

Feature

Multiple Views for a Bigger Picture

The UI adapts based on user needs—interaction reports can be viewed chronologically or grouped by incident, giving Cast flexibility in how they work.

MagicBand scanning

Feature

MagicBands & Media

We enabled managing, scanning, and issuing media and tickets. We collaborated closely with development to ensure quick data transfers and spoke with Cast who scan MagicBands to make the process more efficient.

Simplified form design

Feature

Simplified Data Entry

Creating incident reports was a major pain point. We redesigned the flow to capture guest experiences (good and bad) correctly and efficiently—eliminating the 11-dropdown nightmare.

My Role

Lead Product Designer

I was a lead product designer working on Disney's guest service application. I led design on the Guest Interaction Tracking section, conducted user research with front-line, guest services, and call center staff, and participated in sketch boarding and user testing sessions. We started as 2 lead designers and grew the team to 4.

Lead Product Designer User Research Interaction Design Responsive Design Prototyping User Testing

Impact

Built collaboratively, adopted enthusiastically

The initial launch was met with extreme excitement by Cast Members. We delivered a product that simplified their workflow because we involved them throughout the process.

Reflection

What I learned

The success of the app came from involving Cast Members throughout the process—in user research, testing, and working sessions. When you build with your users, not just for them, adoption comes naturally.
