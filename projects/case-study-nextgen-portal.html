Asurion

Next-Gen Portal

Rebuilding the core operating system for 680+ repair stores nationwide—transforming how experts check in customers, manage repairs, and run their business.

Role

Sr. Manager, Product Design

Timeline

2023 – Present

Team

4 Designers

Platform

Web Application

The Challenge

Replacing 10 years of band-aids in under 2

uBreakiFix technicians and experts rely on internal software to do everything—check in customers, diagnose devices, manage repairs, handle payments, and track inventory. The legacy system had been built over a decade with bandaided features, wasn't integrated with Asurion's other systems, and couldn't scale to support growth.

Challenge 01

Slow & Buggy Legacy System

Experts complained the old system was slow, unreliable, and built on outdated tech stacks that couldn't keep pace with business needs.

Challenge 02

Siloed from Asurion Systems

The homegrown app wasn't integrated with Asurion's inventory, supply chain, or other internal applications—creating data inconsistencies and manual workarounds across teams.

Challenge 03

2 Weeks Just to Learn the System

New technicians needed 2 weeks of training before they could work independently—because the system wasn't intuitive and most actions were manual. Changing repair statuses, selecting client programs, applying the right pricing—all required memorizing steps instead of the system guiding you through them.

The Approach

Deep research, early sharing, and relentless focus on jobs to be done

I started by deeply understanding our experts' pain points and the business goals of our franchisee network. I visited stores, interviewed experts, owners, and customers throughout the entire process—not just at the beginning.

I pushed the design, product, and engineering teams to share early prototypes and get feedback fast. We built service blueprints for our most common store experiences with product, operations, and engineering. I led workshops with store experts, owners, executives from both Asurion and uBreakiFix, and operations teams.

Process

Service Blueprinting

Our team built service blueprints for the most common store experiences, mapping the entire customer journey and identifying pain points across product, operations, and engineering touchpoints.

The Solution

A modern platform built for speed, clarity, and scale

We designed and built a completely new application that transforms how stores operate—from customer check-in to repair completion to payment processing.

Single Source of Truth

Built on Microsoft D365 for parts, pricing, availability, and ordering—no more data silos.

Simplified Experience

Clean interface where technicians easily understand what to do—without 2 weeks of training.

Design System Powered

Using Asurion's UI components to reduce dev time and create consistency.

Feature

Better Customer Experiences

Price estimate feature for instant quotes, Pay by Link for upsells and deposits over the phone, and improved search so experts find what they need fast.

Feature

Configurable for New Partners

A platform that can onboard new clients like Amazon and Google, and new programs like AT&T in-store pickup, in days instead of months.

My Role

Leading design from 0 to 1

I was the design lead on this project from the very beginning—taking it from 0 to 1. I built and managed a team of 4 designers while also establishing a culture of sharing, open feedback, and high design craft. I pushed for customer-facing-level quality for our internal tools.

I partnered with operations leaders, product managers, and engineering leads to set the vision, create long and short-term strategies, and improve processes to deliver faster.

Reflection

What I learned

Building a product with a strong foundation—scalable architecture, deep user research with clear jobs to be done, and a modern tech stack—is critical to succeeding in a complex replatforming effort. Equally important: measuring impact as you launch, adapting quickly, and having leadership aligned on design principles from the start.
